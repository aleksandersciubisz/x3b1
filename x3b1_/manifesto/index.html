Manifesto

Time is a fundamental dimension.



Biological limitations of human perception compel us to perceive events and time in a linear manner. I believe that since we have at our disposal advanced predictive algorithms, the traditional linear mental model has ceased to be adequate and represents a limitation that needs to be questioned.



Skillfully using vast data sets offers the possibility of gaining insights into the future. We can now predict trends, behaviors, and phenomena with a precision that would have seemed impossible just a few decades ago. If we can utilize information coming from the future, it means that our understanding of time requires revision.



Reevaluating our perception of time can open new horizons for human creativity and innovation. By adopting more flexible and complex mental models, we can better adjust to the rapidly changing world, utilizing predictive technologies not only to predict the future but also to actively shape the present.



Therefore, the mission of x3b1 is to facilitate embracing a more nuanced understanding of time and to democratize access to predictive algorithms.
I believe this is a crucial step towards a future where we are not merely passive observers of the flow of time, but active participants in shaping it.
